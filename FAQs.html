FAQs

What is the difference between a pinch and a dash?
A "pinch" is a small, informal amount typically held between your thumb and forefinger. A "dash" is a slightly larger amount, usually around 1/16 of a teaspoon.
What is the best way to chop onions?
To chop onions efficiently, cut off ends, peel, halve through the root, make horizontal and vertical slices, then dice. Use a sharp knife and consider refrigerating the onion to reduce tearing.
What is the best way to measure flour?
To measure flour accurately, fluff it with a spoon, gently spoon it into a measuring cup, and level it off by removing excess flour with a straight edge.
How do I substitute ingredients in a recipe?
When substituting ingredients, pick similar ones, watch for flavor changes, think about consistency, consider allergies, know conversion ratios, and start with small changes when experimenting.
How do I store my herbs and spices?
Store herbs and spices in a cool, dry place, away from sunlight and heat, using airtight containers. Label with the purchase date and check for freshness regularly.
What is the best way to tell if meat is cooked through?
To ensure meat is cooked through, use a meat thermometer to check its internal temperature. Different meats have specific temperature guidelines for safe consumption. Insert the thermometer into the thickest part of the meat without touching bone or fat. This method guarantees accurate results and helps prevent undercooking or overcooking.
What is the best way to clean my cutting board?
Clean your cutting board by washing it with hot, soapy water immediately after use. For deeper cleaning, use a solution of one part vinegar to four parts water. Sanitize with a diluted bleach solution if needed. Air-dry or pat it dry with a clean cloth. Regular cleaning helps maintain a safe and hygienic cutting surface.
